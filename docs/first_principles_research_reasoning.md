# First-Principles Research Reasoning

A first-principles research layer does not connect papers because they look
similar. It connects them because they share underlying questions, mechanisms,
variables, constraints, evidence structures, or unresolved gaps. The system
breaks papers into fundamental knowledge atoms and reasons upward toward
field-level insight.

## Shallow Analogy Reasoning

Shallow systems often connect papers because they share keywords, fields, or
surface labels. That can be useful for search, but it can also hide the actual
research logic.

Example:

```text
Paper A studied cancer cells.
Paper B studied cancer cells.
Therefore they are related.
```

That connection is too weak for discovery planning.

## First-Principles Reasoning

AtlasX asks what the paper is actually testing:

- What question is being asked?
- What entity, population, model, or system is involved?
- What input, exposure, intervention, or variable is applied?
- Under what condition?
- What mechanism is proposed?
- What outcome is measured?
- What evidence supports the claim?
- What boundary conditions or assumptions matter?
- What is not reported?
- What gap or next step follows?

The result is a structured atom that can be compared across sources without
pretending that shared vocabulary equals shared meaning.
