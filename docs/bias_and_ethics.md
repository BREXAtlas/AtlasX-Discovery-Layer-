# Bias and Ethics

Discovery reports can introduce bias because the purpose of the report shapes
what is extracted and highlighted.

## Safeguards

- Run confirmation bias checks.
- Preserve source traceability.
- Preserve uncertainty.
- Do not rank researchers unfairly.
- Do not erase author contribution.
- Include minority and emerging research where available.
- Do not overvalue highly cited work only.
- Distinguish data from interpretation.
- Require human expert review before decisions.

## Common Risks

- **Confirmation bias:** The user may ask questions that pull the model toward a
  desired conclusion.
- **Field-selection bias:** A narrow source set can make a field look more
  settled than it is.
- **Citation bias:** Highly cited or prestigious sources may be over-weighted.
- **Access bias:** Paywalled evidence may be underrepresented or represented
  only through abstracts.
- **Overclaiming:** Generated next steps may sound stronger than the evidence
  allows.

AtlasX should make these risks visible rather than hiding them behind polished
reports.
