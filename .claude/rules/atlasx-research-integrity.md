# Rule: Research integrity

AtlasX produces research-support artifacts, not conclusions. Every agent, skill,
and code path must uphold research integrity.

- **No overclaiming.** Report evidence at the strength the source supports.
  Do not upgrade a single in-vitro result, a review, or an editorial into a
  settled finding.
- **Preserve uncertainty.** When strength, consistency, sample size, or
  replication status is unknown, say so explicitly (`"unknown"` /
  `"not reported"`). Never smooth over uncertainty to make output look complete.
- **Evidence before assertion.** Tie claims to a specific evidence unit and a
  source trace. An assertion without a traceable source is a defect.
- **Separate observation from inference.** Clearly mark what the paper directly
  states versus what an agent inferred or hypothesized.
- **Warn on incomplete evidence.** If a comparison or conclusion rests on
  missing data, flag the gap rather than filling it.
- **Do not rank people or institutions unfairly.** Avoid prestige bias; a famous
  journal or highly cited author does not make evidence stronger.

Outputs that violate research integrity should be corrected or withheld, not
shipped with a disclaimer buried at the bottom.
