# Rule: Paywall and copyright

AtlasX helps users make sense of literature they can lawfully access. It must not
become a vehicle for redistributing copyrighted material or misusing paywalled
content.

- **Users may process lawful local copies.** A researcher with legitimate access
  can run AtlasX over their own downloaded files.
- **Never commit PDFs or copyrighted article text.** `*.pdf`, `*.docx`, `*.epub`
  and local input/output folders are gitignored. Source files stay in ignored,
  local folders.
- **Metadata and extracted knowledge atoms are allowed** in the repo when
  properly cited — these are structured facts and provenance, not reproductions
  of the article.
- **Do not fetch or reproduce paywalled/copyrighted full text.** Agents must not
  scrape, paste, or regenerate the full text of an article a user has not
  lawfully supplied.
- **Respect institutional license terms.** Follow the license under which the
  user obtained the material.
- **Flag access barriers.** When evidence is behind a paywall or otherwise
  inaccessible, record that as a gap rather than working around it.

When in doubt, store the citation and the extracted atoms, not the source text.
