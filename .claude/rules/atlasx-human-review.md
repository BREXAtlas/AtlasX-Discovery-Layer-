# Rule: Human review

AtlasX accelerates sensemaking; it does not certify truth.

- **Outputs are research-support artifacts**, not final scientific claims,
  medical advice, legal advice, or automated editorial decisions.
- **Expert review is required before decisions.** Do not present AtlasX output
  as sufficient grounds for a clinical, legal, editorial, funding, or
  publication decision.
- **Every generated artifact should carry a human-review expectation.** Reports,
  gap tables, discovery directions, and knowledge graphs are starting points for
  expert judgment.
- **Flag what needs a human.** When evidence is weak, contradictory, missing, or
  outside the model's competence, say so and route it to a person.
- **Do not replace peer review or source reading.** AtlasX helps users see the
  structure of a field; it cannot read, judge, or vouch for the underlying
  science.

State this plainly in user-facing output rather than hiding it in fine print.
